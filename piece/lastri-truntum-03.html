Truntum Motif

Truntum No. 03

✦ Drawn by hand by Bu Lastri Bayat, Klaten · Java →

The truntum is the motif of love that returns — tiny stars scattered across the cloth, drawn by a mother for her daughter's wedding. Bu Lastri drew every star of this one by hand, over eleven days. There was only ever one. And it has found where it belongs.

Found its person in Amsterdam March 2024

This cloth is no longer for sale — it found someone. But its page stays here, alive, for the person who carries it. Every time they scan the corner of the fabric, they land right here.

A note from its owner

"I found Bu Lastri's truntum on a grey afternoon in Amsterdam. It made the room warmer. I think about the eleven days every time I wear it."

The story in the cloth

The motif of love that returns.

Truntum means "to bloom again." The story goes that a queen, feeling forgotten, began drawing tiny stars on cloth — and her king, watching her quiet devotion, fell for her a second time. Ever since, mothers draw truntum for their daughters' weddings: a wish that love keeps returning.

Bu Lastri learned it from her mother, who learned it from hers. This cloth carried that wish to Amsterdam — where someone now wears a thousand tiny stars drawn by hand, ten minutes from our workshop.
