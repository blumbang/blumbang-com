The story in the cloth

Why the parang never stops.

Look closely and the diagonal never breaks. The old makers drew it that way on purpose — an unbroken line meant an unbroken effort, a continuity passed from hand to hand. It was once worn only by people of the court; drawing it wrong was unthinkable.

Bu Lastri learned it from her mother, who learned it from hers. When she draws the parang, she isn't copying a pattern. She's continuing a line that started long before any of us — and now it continues into your home, wherever that is.
