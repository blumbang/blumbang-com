Every Blumbang garment carries a small QR code. Scan it anywhere in the world — and the garment records that moment, that city, on a living map.

Not where it was made. Where it went. A kaos born in Jeto, Klaten — spotted later in Tokyo, Amsterdam, or Mahé. The story grows every time someone wears it somewhere new.

The people behind it

Meet the makers

01

Bang Arie

Master Screen Printer · since 1998

Twenty-six years of ink and mesh. Bang Arie has been screen printing since before most of our customers owned a phone. Every garment that leaves this workshop passes through his hands.

02

Bu Lastri

Batik Tulis · Bayat, Klaten

From the batik village of Bayat — ten minutes from our workshop. Bu Lastri makes batik tulis the slow way: hand-drawn wax, natural dyes, days of patient work. Not mass produced. Not rushed.

03

Pak Joko

Stone Craft · Lereng Merapi

Volcanic stone from the slopes of Mount Merapi, shaped into basins and surfaces by craftsmen who've worked this material for generations. Heavy, honest, made to last centuries.
