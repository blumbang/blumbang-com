Bu Lastri

She draws batik tulis by hand — wax, canting, natural dye. The slow way her mother taught her, and her mother before that.

Batik Tulis Natural Dye ~4 pieces a month Since (year)

Bu Lastri lives in Bayat, a batik village about ten minutes from the Blumbang workshop. We didn't find her on a marketplace. We've known her family for years.

Every piece begins as plain cloth and a small copper canting. She heats the wax, and then — line by line, by hand, no stencil, no machine — she draws. A single piece takes about eleven days.

"Sabar wae, mengko rak dadi."
"Just be patient — it'll come together in the end." The way she answers when someone asks why it takes so long.

When you take home one of her pieces, more than half of what you pay goes straight to her hands. We don't take much. The shirts are how Blumbang lives — this is how we give something back.

Bu Lastri takes commissions — a motif that means something to you, in the colours you choose. We'll arrange a time for the two of you to talk, and bridge the language if you need it.

We set up the meeting so it works for both sides — no cold calls, no pressure. Just two people, introduced properly.
